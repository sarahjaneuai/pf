The artifact I chose for the professional use of language is a cover letter that I wrote in a class to apply for my dream job. Since my dream job is to be a Chief Financial Officer (CFO), I wrote the letter as if I were applying to be the CFO for the company I currently work at, 12 years in the future. It has to be in the future because at the moment, I am nowhere near qualified for that job. I blacked out some of the information because I felt that it was sensitive and revealing it was not necessary for the purpose of the letter. This cover letter demonstrates the criteria for this section because I wrote it using a very professional tone and discussed my chosen profession (Accounting) in Portuguese, showing at least a basic understanding of the specialized terminology in my second language. In a professional sense, I believe I have developed a lot during my time at BYU through the different experiences I have had, including working on a published research website, completing an internship abroad, and doing my own research on industry-specific jargon for my future in Accounting. To continue to use and develop my skills in this area, I plan to make all my future employers aware that I speak Portuguese and ask for opportunities to work with international clients, specifically those from Lusophone countries. My goal is to be able to go on different business trips to Portuguese-speaking countries so I can network and learn more from native speakers.

For more of my professional use of the language, click here to see the Portuguese LinkedIn profile I made.

For my personal use of language, I chose to use a sonnet I wrote as my artifact. This is a creative work that I wrote about being heartbroken after a past relationship ended. It demonstrates my ability to think and express myself creatively in my own life, which is why it is a perfect artifact for the personal use of language. In being a Portuguese Studies secondary major at BYU, I have developed a lot of good skills for my own personal use of the language. I am still able to converse with my friends in Brasil even though I have not been there in over 3 years, and I am able to connect with my peers a t BYU through Portuguese and how much I care about it. I am of the opinion that the best way to continue to develop personal skills, I need to keep talking to my friends! As I use Portuguese in my personal life, I will learn how to speak better and I will be able to increase my connection with others all throughout the world. I also would like to teach my future children to speak Portuguese, which is something that BYU has helped me feel more equipped to do by teaching me a lot of the linguistic and grammatical rules. I am married to a fellow Portuguese-speaker, so my plan is to continue to practice with him throughout our lives so that we may both improve our skills, especially in a personal setting, so that the two of us can both continue to keep in touch with our friends in Brasil and Portugal.
